Understanding Aadhaar OVSE

Aadhaar OVSE (Offline Verification Seeking Entity) is a certification granted by the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI) to organizations that wish to verify Aadhaar numbers and associated identity credentials without connecting to the Central Identity Data Repository (CIDR). This framework enables consent-based, secure offline verification through digitally signed credentials.

Definition & Framework: The OVSE framework is built on four fundamental principles that guide its implementation and regulatory compliance.

Technical Process Flow

The offline verification process follows a clearly defined workflow that ensures security while maintaining user convenience.

1

User Opens App

Aadhaar holder accesses the new Aadhaar App on their mobile device with internet or offline capability.

2

Scans QR Code

User scans QR code generated by OVSE specifying which credentials are being requested.

3

Face Verification

Optional face capture and matching against Aadhaar photo ensures proof of presence. Can be skipped based on OVSE settings.

4

Shares Credentials

Digitally signed verifiable credentials containing requested data transmitted securely to OVSE system.

5

Verification Complete

OVSE validates cryptographic signatures and stores verification record without connecting to UIDAI CIDR.

Infrastructure Requirements: OVSEs require secure callback URL endpoints, valid SSL/TLS certificates (minimum 2 years validity), and systems capable of receiving and validating digitally signed credentials. Organizations can operate completely offline in remote locations with pre-generated QR codes and cached verification lists.

Challenges & Solutions

Implementing OVSE technology presents both challenges and opportunities. The framework has evolved to address earlier concerns through regulatory amendments and technical improvements.

âš  Challenge: Document Fraud & Forgery
Traditional physical Aadhaar copies were prone to falsification and unauthorized duplication, creating identity theft risks and enabling fraudulent access to services.
âœ“ Solution: Cryptographic Verification
OVSE digitally signs all credentials with UIDAI-provided cryptographic keys, making forgery mathematically impossible and enabling real-time authenticity validation.
âš  Challenge: Internet Dependency
Rural and remote locations lack consistent broadband connectivity, preventing residents from accessing identity verification services and modern financial services.
âœ“ Solution: Complete Offline Capability
Both users and OVSEs can function completely offline after initial QR code generation. Pre-generated codes enable verification in areas without any connectivity.
âš  Challenge: Unauthorized Data Sharing
Users had limited control over which personal information was shared, raising privacy concerns and regulatory compliance issues under the DPDP Act.
âœ“ Solution: Selective Credential Sharing
Users now generate customized QR codes sharing only specific fields (name, DOB, photo) as required. Organizations cannot access data not explicitly shared.
âš  Challenge: Proof of Presence
Without physical presence verification, proxy users could conduct transactions on behalf of actual residents, enabling fraud and impersonation.
âœ“ Solution: Face Verification
New Aadhaar App includes advanced face recognition matching stored Aadhaar photograph with real-time capture, ensuring the legitimate individual is conducting the transaction.
